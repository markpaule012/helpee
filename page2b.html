Bigay Puso sa Pasko 2018

Bigay Puso sa Pasko (English translation: Give Your Heart on Christmas) is an annual Christmas party for indigent kids in the Payatas community.

WHERE THE FUNDS GO

All donations received from this campaign would go to funding this outreach event.

BACKGROUND

Built on a landfill, Payatas remains as the largest open dumpsite in the Philippines and is one of the most poverty-stricken areas in the Metro Manila area. Although Payatas has been classified by the Department of Education as a disadvantaged, depressed, and underserved (DDU) urban area, and absenteeism and malnutrition are persistent and severe problems for most of its local students, the kids of Payatas are still so fun and genuinely happy all the time. The project was founded in 2011 at Lupang Pangako Elementary School in Payatas, Quezon City by teacher Shirley E. Alambra after she noticed the lack of excitement and joy in her students at their previous Christmas parties, some eating sardines as their only holiday meal. With the help of sponsors, she was able to start providing 100 kids with groceries, toys, and a Jollibee meal each year at the annual Christmas party (with a special appearance from the Bee himself). Thanks to the increasing support and generosity of donors over the years, Bigay Puso sa Pasko has grown to sponsor more than double the number of kids as when it originally began. Last year, Bigay Puso sa Pasko expanded to two different locations, Lupang Pangako Elementary School and West Fairview Elementary School, to accommodate 500 students. This year's Bigay Puso sa Pasko event will be held for 300 kids in the Payatas community on Sunday, December 16, 2018. Approximately $6 will provide a grocery package, toy, and Jollibee meal for one student, and anything in excess will be donated towards the local community. Thank you so much in advance, especially to the friends and family who have continually supported this project throughout the years. None of this is possible without you. Happy holidays! Love, Kym
